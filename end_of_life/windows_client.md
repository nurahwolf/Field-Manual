### Useful Information

- **General Availability Channel (GAC)**: The regular update channel. Regular updates, and reaches `End of Servicing` in two to four years after release.
	- Used by `Home`, `Professional` and `Enterprise`.
	- Generally the `End of Servicing` date is the same for both support and security updates.
		- Generally supported for 2 years for `Home and Professional` since release, or 4 years for `Enterprise`.
- **Long Term Servicing Channel (LTSC)**: An extended support update channel. Updates are less frequent, and usually supported for up to 10 years.
	- Used by `Enterprice LTSC` and `Enterprice IoT LTSC` only.
	- Usually has a `Mainstream Support`, `Security Support` and `Extended Support` date.
		- `Extended Support` is not always available. Usually its the 'last' ever build of that version (e.g. Windows 10)
	- Users are often unaware that security patches that can have 'operational impact' are disabled by default. This means things like SeriousSAM, Meltdown/Spectra and other well known issues have to be **manually configured**.
